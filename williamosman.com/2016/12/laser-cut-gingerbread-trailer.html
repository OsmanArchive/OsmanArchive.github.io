Laser Cut Gingerbread Trailer

You don't need a house to have a home... Here's a glorious, burnt, laser cut, gingerbread trailer.

Why?

Why not? 

I don't really have a good answer.

Design

First we need inspiration.
Home sweet home
Shitter's full

Next comes a CAD model textured with the SolidWorks sand preset. I tinted it a little brown.
Curved faces
I intended on baking the curved cookie over little forms, but kinda forgot.
Straight curves and swamp cooler
Three flat rectangles can approximate a curve!


My second backing attempt rendered structurally sound gingerbread sheets. These were cut on the laser.
What a waste of technology
Nobody actually eats gingerbread houses right? 

No?

Sweet! Hot glue 'dries' way faster than frosting! 

Turns out hot glue works only OK; it holds fast but tends to rip the top layer of cookie off.
Blasphemy
Problem:
The CAD model assumed a 3/16 inch thick cookie, but in reality it was 1/4 inch. A few of the pieces need to be trimmed down.
Too wide
Another problem with hot glue is it covers your Christmas decoration in faux spiderwebs.
This isn't Halloween damnit!
Royal icing was used to reinforce the trailer and hide the spiderwebs.

Turns out I have no idea how to decorate a gingerbread house. I realized this about halfway through decorating a gingerbread house.
Gross candy!
This little trailer park may actually have benefited from my inferior exterior decorating skills.

Conclusion

Unsurprisingly, gingerbread cuts well on the laser. What did surprise me was the smell this thing imparted on food in my refrigerator.... Everything has absorbed this burnt gingerbread smell/taste; especially the butter.
